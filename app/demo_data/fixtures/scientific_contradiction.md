# Effect of Compound X on Cellular Senescence

## Abstract

We report that treatment with Compound X **significantly reduces** markers of
cellular senescence in primary fibroblasts across all three donor lines tested
(p < 0.01 for all comparisons). These results suggest Compound X may have
therapeutic potential in age-related disease.

## Methods

Primary human dermal fibroblasts from three donor lines (D1, D2, D3) were
treated with Compound X (10 µM) or vehicle control for 72 hours. Senescence
was quantified by β-galactosidase staining and p16/p21 western blot.

## Results

| Donor | Vehicle β-gal+ | Compound X β-gal+ | p-value |
|-------|----------------|-------------------|---------|
| D1    | 38%            | 41%               | 0.42    |
| D2    | 35%            | 39%               | 0.31    |
| D3    | 42%            | 44%               | 0.58    |

Across all three donor lines, Compound X treatment was associated with a
**modest increase** in β-galactosidase-positive cells (not statistically
significant). Western blot analysis showed no significant change in p16 or
p21 protein levels (Figure 2).

## Discussion

Our data do not support a senolytic effect of Compound X at the
concentrations and timepoints tested. The discrepancy with prior literature
may reflect differences in donor genetic background or cell-passage number.
