# AlphaTrial — Phase II Study of Compound X

## Trial overview

AlphaTrial was a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase II study
designed to evaluate the efficacy and tolerability of Compound X in adults
presenting with moderate symptom burden on the standardized rating scale.

## Methodology

- **Enrolment:** 120 patients, randomized 1:1 to treatment (n=60) or placebo
  (n=60).
- **Inclusion criteria:** age 18–65, baseline severity score ≥ 14, no
  contraindicating comorbidities.
- **Duration:** 8 weeks of double-blind treatment, followed by a 4-week
  open-label extension.
- **Schedule:** study drug or matching placebo was administered once daily
  in the morning, with food.
- **Blinding:** identical-appearance capsules were prepared by an independent
  pharmacy. Investigators, patients, and outcome assessors remained blinded
  through database lock.

## Primary endpoint

The primary endpoint was the change from baseline in the standardized
severity score at week 8.

- Treatment group: mean improvement **14.2%** (95% CI 11.1–17.3).
- Placebo group:   mean improvement **3.1%**  (95% CI 1.2–5.0).
- Between-group difference: **11.1 percentage points** (p < 0.001).

## Secondary endpoints

Quality-of-life measures (EQ-5D) improved more in the treatment arm at
week 8 (Δ +0.08 vs +0.02; p = 0.014). Patient-reported global impression
of change favored treatment.

## Safety

- Total adverse events: 6% in treatment, 5% in placebo (p = 0.78).
- Serious adverse events: 1 in treatment (mild reversible hepatotoxicity,
  resolved at follow-up), 0 in placebo.
- Discontinuations: 2 in treatment (one for AE, one withdrew consent), 1 in
  placebo (relocation).

## Conclusion

Compound X demonstrated a statistically and clinically meaningful
improvement over placebo on the primary endpoint at week 8, with a
favorable tolerability profile. A confirmatory Phase III trial is in
planning.
